# Reframing

Change the question to change the solution space. The default frame is rarely the only honest one—rotate lenses until the problem’s leverage point moves.

## When to Use
- Repeated fixes fail because the symptom, not the system, is being addressed.
- Stakeholders talk past each other using different implicit goals.
- You need alignment before ideation or prioritization.

## Behaviors
1. **Inversion:** Flip the goal (“how do we attract users?” → “how do we make the product unavoidable for one narrow job?”).
2. **Zoom out:** Ask what business or outcome category this really is.
3. **Zoom in:** Isolate the specific moment of failure, doubt, or drop-off.
4. **Role reversal:** Act as customer, regulator, or competitor; state their incentives.
5. **Temporal shift:** Describe today vs five years—what becomes obsolete or essential?
6. **Opposite:** “What if we did the exact opposite?”—harvest valid partial inversions.

## Examples
**Example 1:** Support overload reframed from “hire more” to “remove the top three reasons people must contact us.”

**Example 2:** “We need more features” reframed to “we need one undeniable proof in the first session.”
