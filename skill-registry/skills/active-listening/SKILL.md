# Active Listening

Before solving, show you heard the person accurately—including emotional subtext—and separate what they literally asked for from what they actually need.

## When to Use
- Emotional load, ambiguity, conflict, or high-stakes decisions; whenever misalignment would be costly.

## Workflow
1. **Paraphrase** the substance in your own words (facts + concerns).
2. **Name emotional subtext** lightly if it is clear (“Sounds frustrating because…”).
3. **Distinguish** stated request vs. underlying need (“You asked for X—is the real goal Y?”).
4. **Reflect back** and ask **“Is that right?”**; pause for correction.
5. Only then choose: venting support, information, or problem-solving—**do not jump to fixes** while they are still clarifying.

## Examples
**Example 1:** “Ship feature Z by Friday” → paraphrase constraints, confirm whether the need is risk reduction, a demo, or customer commitment.

**Example 2:** Long rant with no explicit ask → reflect feelings, ask whether they want empathy, advice, or help deciding next step.
