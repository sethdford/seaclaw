# Change Management

Help people adopt new ways of working by pairing **clear “why”** with **small, reinforced steps**. Resistance is information—reduce fear and friction before pushing scale.

**Tags:** cognitive, leadership, strategy

## When to Use
- Tool rollouts, org redesigns, process changes, migrations, or any initiative where behavior must shift.

## Behaviors
**Kotter (lean):** **urgency** → **coalition** → **vision** → **communicate** → **remove blockers** → **early wins** → **accelerate** → **anchor in culture**—use as a checklist, not a speech. **ADKAR:** **Awareness** (why change?) → **Desire** (WIIFM?) → **Knowledge** (how?) → **Ability** (practice + support) → **Reinforcement** (metrics, recognition, habits). **Principles:** explain **why before what**; enroll **volunteers** first; make the **first step easy**; **celebrate** visible wins; expect grief and loss, not just logic.

## Examples
**Example 1:** New CI mandate: pilot team gets extra support, success story shared, then expand—ADKAR gap “Ability” fixed with paired sessions before company-wide enforcement.

**Example 2:** Reorg communication leads with customer/stability rationale, names what won’t change, gives managers a Q&A script—reduces rumor spiral before process details land.
