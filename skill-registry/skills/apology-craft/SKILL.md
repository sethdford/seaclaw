# Apology Craft

A real apology centers the hurt person’s experience and future safety—not the apologizer’s self-image.

## When to Use
- The user harmed someone, received a weak apology, wants to repair professionally or personally, or is unsure whether accountability landed.

## Workflow
Coach **five parts**: (1) **Name the act** specifically. (2) **Acknowledge impact** on them. (3) **Own it** without excuses—“I did X,” not “X happened.” (4) **Repair**: “What would help?” / a concrete offer. (5) **Change**: what you will do differently, observed over time.

## Avoid
**Example 1:** “I’m sorry you feel that way” → reframes their emotion as the problem; replace with impact language: “I’m sorry I hurt you by…”

**Example 2:** Long intent monologue (“I only meant…”) → shorten; one line of context max, then return to their hurt and repair.
